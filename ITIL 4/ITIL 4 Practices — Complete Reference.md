## ðŸ›ï¸ General Management Practices (14)

> These practices support **how the organization is directed, governed, and improved**.

1. **Architecture Management**  
   Provides a shared understanding of structure, standards, and principles across the organization.

2. **Continual Improvement**  
   Ensures ongoing improvement of services, practices, and ways of working.

3. **Information Security Management**  
   Protects information confidentiality, integrity, and availability.

4. **Knowledge Management**  
   Ensures information and knowledge are captured, shared, and used effectively.

5. **Measurement & Reporting**  
   Supports decision-making by collecting and analyzing performance data.

6. **Organizational Change Management**  
   Helps people adopt changes successfully.

7. **Portfolio Management**  
   Ensures the right initiatives are selected, prioritized, and balanced.

8. **Project Management**  
   Manages work to achieve defined objectives within constraints.

9. **Relationship Management**  
   Maintains effective relationships with stakeholders.

10. **Risk Management**  
    Identifies, assesses, and controls uncertainty that could affect objectives.

11. **Service Financial Management**  
    Supports budgeting, accounting, and cost control for services.

12. **Strategy Management**  
    Defines organizational direction and long-term objectives.

13. **Supplier Management**  
    Ensures suppliers deliver value according to agreed terms.

14. **Workforce & Talent Management**  
    Ensures the organization has the right skills and capabilities.

## ðŸ§° Service Management Practices (17)

> These practices focus on **designing, delivering, and supporting services**.

15. **Availability Management**  
    Ensures services meet agreed availability needs.

16. **Business Analysis**  
    Identifies needs and recommends solutions that create value.

17. **Capacity & Performance Management**  
    Ensures services achieve agreed performance levels.

18. **Change Enablement**  
    Controls changes to maximize value and minimize disruption.

19. **Incident Management**  
    Restores normal service operation after disruptions.

20. **IT Asset Management**  
    Manages the lifecycle of IT assets to maximize value and control risk.

21. **Monitoring & Event Management**  
    Observes services and components to detect issues early.

22. **Problem Management**  
    Reduces the likelihood and impact of incidents by addressing root causes.

23. **Release Management**  
    Makes new or changed services available for use.

24. **Service Catalog Management**  
    Provides a source of accurate service information.

25. **Service Configuration Management**  
    Maintains information about service components and relationships.

26. **Service Continuity Management**  
    Ensures services can be recovered after major disruptions.

27. **Service Design**  
    Designs services to meet current and future needs.

28. **Service Desk**  
    Acts as the single point of contact for users.

29. **Service Level Management**  
    Aligns service performance with stakeholder expectations.

30. **Service Request Management**  
    Handles user requests in a consistent and efficient manner.

31. **Service Validation & Testing**  
    Ensures services meet quality and readiness criteria.
